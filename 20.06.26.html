Last day of Spring

It's the 20th June and I reflect on what has been an exciting spring, full of hope for the remainder of the year. I have done lots of walks, cycled to Cambridge, made a friend, and created the Outpost radio station. Plus in addition, I have pulled all of my PA together to produce a good rig, something I am really pleased with. I finally got to use the roof tent and went camping, at the 11th hour got rid of all, well most of my garden rubbish. Enough to make a serious impact on my encouragement of finishing the garden. I booked Lucas and I's adventure in detail to Cornwall and supported his summer bucket list. I made this site, by far the site I am most proud of, it is still a work in progress and I will endeavour to continue to improve it because that is what I like to do. I am really happy with it though. I guess finally, I have up'd my game with recording podcasts and created an efficient method of recording, coding and publishing. I proved to myself that I can do all of these things from both a roof tent, a hotel room and my car.

Tomorrow is my birthday and the first day of summer, I still have some work to do but I am excited about it. I am going to officially start my training for JogLe on the first of July, giving me a full year to improve my cycling and fund raising. I plan to ride to various places around the south of England, stay over, podcast and then come back. Today, part of my celebration, Lucas and I are going into London to ride around Hyde park and listen to music, then we are off to the museum of illusions on Tottenham court road. I will update you.
